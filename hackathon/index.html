Earn Additional Income with Carbon Credits

A simple guide for our farmers on how your hard work improves the soil and pays you extra.

1. What is a Carbon Credit?

Think of it as a **certificate for storing pollution**. When you farm in a way that pulls carbon dioxide (a pollutant) out of the air and traps it in your soil, you earn one "Carbon Credit" for every tonne of CO2 you store.

Companies that create pollution buy this credit from you to balance out their impact. **You get paid for cleaning the air and making your soil healthier.**

2. How Your Farming Generates Credits

Regenerative (or residue-free) farming is all about building healthy, living soil. When you use practices like **no-till farming**, planting **cover crops**, and using our **residue-free bio-inputs** (instead of chemical fertilizers), you are feeding the soil. This process of trapping carbon in the soil is called "carbon sequestration."

3. How We Build "Soil Organic Carbon" (SOC)

Healthy soil is like a giant sponge for carbon. Plant roots and microorganisms take carbon from the air (through photosynthesis) and store it deep in the soil as stable "organic matter." The more organic matter, the higher your Soil Organic Carbon (SOC), the healthier your soil, and the more carbon credits you can earn.
